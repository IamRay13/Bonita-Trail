Essential Hiking Gear (Must Bring)

  • Hiking boots or trail shoes
  • Backpack (lightweight, comfortable)
  • Water (min. 2L/person)
  • Weather-appropriate clothing (layers for warmth, moisture-wicking materials)
  • Map/GPS device or trail guide
  • Small first-aid kit
  • Knife/Multi-tool
  • Headlamp/Flashlight + batteries
  • Personal hygiene items
  • Trash bag (Leave No Trace!)
  • Sunscreen
  • Snacks/Energy bars
  • Emergency whistle
  • Rain gear (poncho or waterproof jacket)
  • Hat & sunglasses for sun protection

Hiking Attire Checklist

  • Moisture-wicking top (synthetic or wool blend)
  • Breathable hiking pants or shorts (comfortable)
  • Lightweight jacket or fleece (for cooler hours)
  • Hiking boots (well broken-in)
  • Extra hiking socks (moisture-wicking)
  • Sun hat or cap
  • Sunglasses
  • Rain gear (waterproof poncho or jacket)
  • Bandana/Buff (optional)

Trail Tips & Reminders

  • Leave No Trace: Pack out everything you pack in, including food scraps and trash. Stay on marked trails.
  • Hydration is Key: Drink water regularly, even if you don't feel thirsty, especially during the hike.
  • Sun Protection: The sun can be intense, even on cloudy days. Use sunscreen, wear a hat, and sunglasses.
  • Footwear: Ensure your hiking boots are well broken-in to prevent blisters. Bring blister treatment just in case.
  • Check Gear: Test your tent, stove (if bringing), and headlamp before leaving home.
  • Inform Someone: Let someone not on the trip know your itinerary and expected return time.
  • Weather Awareness: Mountain weather can change quickly. Be prepared for cooler temperatures at night and potential rain. (Check the forecast!)
  • Enjoy the Journey: Take time to appreciate the views, the company, and the experience!
